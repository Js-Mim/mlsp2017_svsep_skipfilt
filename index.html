Overview:

This webpage serves as a demonstration for our work in singing voice source separation via gated recurrent units (GRU) and skip-filtering connections. Practically, we propose a method to directly learn time-frequency masks from observed mixture magnitude spectra. This is realized via supervised training, using prior knowledge only of the target magnitude spectrum and not of any time-frequency mask.

Audio Demo & Comparison with Previous Approaches :

We compare with state-of-the-art methods dealing with monaural singing voice separation. More specifically, we compare the results from our proposed method against:


i) Two deep feed-forward neural networks, for predicting both binary and soft time-frequency masks, denoted as GRA2 [2] and GRA3 [3] .

ii) A convolutional encoder-decoder approach, for estimating a soft time-frequency mask and denoted as CHA [4] .

iii) Two denoising auto-encoders operating on the common fate signal representation [5] denoted as STO1, STO2 .
